    Multiple Input Sources for Agricultural Monitoring

    To ensure greater flexibility and comprehensiveness in monitoring agricultural conditions, the project relies on several sources of visual data input. These diverse methods work together to provide a holistic overview of crop health, enabling better decision-making and timely interventions.

    Drones

    High-Resolution Aerial Imaging and Real-Time Data Transmission

    Drones equipped with high-resolution cameras and sensors fly over the agricultural fields regularly to capture detailed images and videos. These aerial shots provide a bird’s-eye view of the crops, covering large areas quickly and with precision.

    Mobile Phones

    Manual Visual Input from Farmers and Field Support Teams

    Farmers and field agents use their smartphones to take photos or videos of crops, especially when they notice unusual conditions or symptoms in specific plants. This manual input enriches the data pool with on-the-ground observations.

    Uploading Pre-Recorded Videos

    Analysis of Previously Captured Visual Data from Various Sources

    The system allows users to upload pre-recorded videos and images collected from other sources, such as past drone flights, CCTV cameras, or third-party surveys. This functionality adds historical context and extra data for trend analysis.

    Aerial Monitoring Technology Using Drones

    How Does This Technology Work?

    Drones use high-resolution cameras and advanced sensors to monitor agricultural crops from the air. These drones fly over fields regularly, capturing detailed images of every part of the land, providing visual data that helps analyze plant health.

    Early Disease Detection

    Aerial images reveal subtle changes in plant color or shape, indicating disease or pest infestation before it spreads.

    Comprehensive Coverage

    Drones can scan large areas of farmland quickly, with higher accuracy than traditional ground monitoring.

    Time and Labor Savings

    They eliminate the need for manual field inspections of every plant, reducing labor costs and improving efficiency.

    Smart Data Analysis

    Images are integrated with AI systems to generate automatic reports on risk areas and treatment suggestions.

    • Texture differences (dryness, mold, insect damage)
